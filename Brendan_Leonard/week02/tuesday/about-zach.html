Zach grew up in Chicago to a lamaze instructor mother and a professional story telling father. An unconventional childhood but one that fueled his creative passions and artistic drive from an early age.

As a child he loved nothing more than visiting art museums and was a keen drawer and sculptor. That continued after high school when he attended Hunter College, part of the City University of New York and one of America's finest art schools.

Graduating in 2000 he bluffed his way into a web design job with no technical skills whatsoever. He became obsessed with computer animation and taught himself Flash and ActionScript from books.

He decided to go all-in on programming and attended post graduate studies at Parsons School of Design. He stayed on and taught at Parsons for 10 years; eventually becoming a professor.

The non-teaching parts of the job got him down and so he quit and started his own school called the School for Poetic Computation.

One of Zach's most import contributions to computer science has been openFrameworks, a tool for artists. It's an open source C++ toolkit that is used to create a framework of easy-to-use software tools for artists to use.

Zach is also the driving force behind the inspirational EyeWriter. A low-cost system that has enabled a famous LA gravity artist, Tempt1, who is now completely physically paralyzed due to a form of motor neurone disease, to create art by simply moving his eyes.

In addition to all of the above he also runs a creative studio with his wife, Molmol Kuo, an accomplished commercial artist. His other big passion is using his coding skills to create unbelievable electronic music. He performed at music festivals in Europe for many years.
